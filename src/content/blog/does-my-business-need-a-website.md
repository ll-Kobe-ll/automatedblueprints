Short answer: yes, and probably more than you think.

Here is the part most owners miss. When someone hears your name from a friend, the first thing they do is Google you. Not to find your number. To check if you are real. They want to see that you exist, that you do good work, and that you are still in business.

If nothing comes up, or all they find is a Facebook page that has not been touched in two years, a lot of them quietly move on. You never hear about it. The referral your buddy gave you just booked someone else because that company looked more legit online.

## "But all my work comes from word of mouth"

That is great, and it is exactly why a website matters more for you, not less. Word of mouth gets your name into someone's head. A website is what they check before they call. The two work together. Right now you are doing the hard part (earning the referral) and then losing some of those people at the easy part (looking real online).

A website does not replace word of mouth. It protects it.

## What your site actually needs to do

You do not need anything fancy. Most local businesses overthink this. A site that works is one that:

- **Loads fast on a phone.** Most people will look you up on their phone, often standing in their kitchen or sitting in a truck. If it is slow or hard to read, they bounce.
- **Says what you do and where, in plain words.** No clever taglines. "Garage door repair in Orange County" beats "Opening doors to excellence."
- **Has one tap to call.** The whole point is to turn a visitor into a phone call. Make the button obvious.
- **Shows you are trustworthy.** A few reviews, a license number, real photos of real work. That is what closes the gap between "found them" and "called them."
- **Shows up when people search.** Built so Google can actually read it and rank it for what you do.

That is it. A clean, fast, honest site beats a flashy one every time.

## You are probably ahead of your competition already

Here is the good news. Most of your local competitors have nothing real online either. A dead Facebook page. A free site builder thing they abandoned. So the bar is low. A proper site does not just keep you in the running, it puts you ahead of the other guys who never bothered.

You cannot answer every call. You are on a job, under a sink, up a ladder, or driving. The call goes to voicemail and you figure you will call back when you get a minute.

Here is the problem. The customer does not wait. They found you on Google, which means they found three of your competitors on the same screen. If you do not pick up, the next number is one tap away. Most people who hit your voicemail never leave a message, and a good chunk of them have booked someone else before you even notice the missed call.

## What missed call text-back actually does

It is exactly what it sounds like. When a call comes in that you do not answer, the customer automatically gets a text back within about a minute. Something simple and human, like:

> Sorry we missed your call. This is [Your Business]. What can we help you with?

That one text changes everything, because now the customer is in a conversation with you instead of dialing the next company. The urgency to keep shopping disappears. They reply, tell you what they need, and you call them back when you are free, already knowing what the job is.

## Why it works so well

Three reasons:

- **Speed wins jobs.** The business that responds first usually gets the work. A text in under a minute means you are first, even when you could not pick up.
- **It feels personal, not automated.** You approve the wording ahead of time, so it reads like a real person from your shop sent it.
- **It costs almost nothing to run.** Compared to what one lost job is worth, this is the cheapest insurance you can buy against missed calls.

## A real example

A plumber is under a house replacing a drain line. His phone buzzes twice. The old way, he climbs out an hour later to two voicemails and finds out both callers already booked someone else.

With text-back running, both callers got a message within a minute. One replied "water heater leaking, how soon can you come?" He booked a same-week job without ever touching his phone while he was working. That is a job he would have lost, recovered automatically.

## How to set it up

You keep your existing number. Calls still ring your phone like normal. The system just watches for the ones that go unanswered and fires the text. You see every reply in one simple inbox, so nothing falls through.
